I did some writings with TCI and GPT-2

Ted Nelson had vast visions for his Hyperlinks but is very disappointed with its current stage. A hyperlink wasn't meant to be a jump link, taking you from one place to another. It was meant to show some content while still seeing its source. Ted Nelson's visions inspire this tool for Hypertext, hyperlinks, and his software Xanadu.  This is a writing tool that lets the user bring in their references. They can feed found texts to the OpenAir text generator GPT-2. The generator will give the user a new text based on the input but with new meaning.

The idea is that the user then will add their own words to the text. The user can add the nonlinear space of their mind itself. This space can even be considered a representation of the brain. Connecting other references while adding on a layer of the mind. Old material is leveraged by a computer and edited by a human. New content will be created.
